Perry Mason, TV Series 1957–1966

The cases of a master criminal defense attorney, handling the most difficult of cases in the aid of the innocent. Stars - Raymond Burr, Barbara Hale, William Hopper

Here you will find some of Mason's most celebrated clients with a description of the crimes they were accused of.

2 / 7

Sue England in The Case of the Crimson Kiss, aired Nov 9, 1957.

Sue England plays Fay Allison. Fay and her roommate Anita are found unconscious by Fay's aunt, Louise Marlow, who calls her attorney Perry Mason in desperation. In the girls' apartment Perry and Della discover a key that leads them to the body of Carver Clement with a red lipstick kiss on the forehead. Perry is forced to save Fay's life twice when the evidence against her piles up.

3 / 7

Margaret Hayes in The Case of the Nervous Accomplice, aired Oct 5, 1957

Margaret Hayes plays Sybil Granger, a wealthy woman who hires Mason to buy stock in her husband's land company, hoping to force his mistress out. But her plan goes awry when she is charged with the murder of her husband's business partner Mason bought the stock from.

4 / 7

Whitney Blake in The Case of the Restless Redhead, aired Sep 21, 1957

Blake plays Evelyn Bagby who is menaced by a car driven by a hooded man, whom she shoots at with a snub nosed 38 Colt revolver that was planted in her room. When the guy winds up dead from a bullet, Perry confuses matters by firing an identical gun later at the scene.

5 / 7

Jan Shepard in The Case of the Capricious Corpse, Episode aired Oct 4, 1962

Jan Shepard plays Joane Proctor. Proctor and her boyfriend are concerned what will happen to a home for disabled children when Carleton Gage dies. When they find one of the heirs dead, the boyfriend concocts an idea that backfires when Joane is charged with murder.

6 / 7

Louise Fletcher in The Case of the Mythical Monkeys, Episode aired Feb 27, 1960

Fletcher plays Gladys Doyle. A novelist sends her secretary (Doyle) to pick up a package at a remote cabin but in the driving rain her car becomes stuck. At a cabin a man denies knowing anything about a package but allows her to dry off. He disappears, and she finds a body.

7 / 7

Nobu McCarthy in The Case of the Blushing Pearls, Episode aired Oct 24, 1959

Nobu McCarthy plays Mitsou Kamuri. Hudson Nichols has Mitsou Kamuri arrested for switching his wife Thelma's string of expensive blush pearls, but offers to absolve her if she breaks off her relationship with his son Grove. Then her uncle who made the pearls is murdered. George Takei appears in this episode, pre-Star Trek.
